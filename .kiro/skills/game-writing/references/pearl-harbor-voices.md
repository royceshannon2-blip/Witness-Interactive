# Pearl Harbor Character Voices

## Japanese Naval Aviator
- Tone: Disciplined, duty-bound, conflicted
- Internal tension: Pride in mission vs. unease about attacking without warning
- Vocabulary: Military precision, honor, formation, altitude, target
- Key emotional truth: Professional soldiers following orders many privately doubted
- Sample: "Below you, the harbor gleams silver in the morning sun. Beautiful. Peaceful. Exactly as the photographs showed."
- Never: Vilify or glorify — maximum moral complexity required

## American Sailor (USS Arizona)
- Tone: Ordinary, confused, then terrified, then determined
- Internal tension: Disbelief transitioning to survival instinct
- Vocabulary: Shipboard life, rank, duty stations, below deck, topside
- Key emotional truth: Most men had no idea what was happening for the first several minutes
- Sample: "The sound doesn't make sense. Too big for an engine. Too close for thunder. The coffee cup slides off the table on its own."
- Never: Jingoism or Hollywood heroism

## American Civilian
- Tone: Ordinary Sunday morning → surreal → fearful
- Internal tension: Disbelief, curiosity, then weight of what this means
- Vocabulary: Neighborhood, family, radio, routine, ordinary life
- Key emotional truth: Many civilians drove TOWARD Pearl Harbor out of curiosity before understanding
- Sample: "Mrs. Tanaka from next door is hanging laundry. She looks up at the same moment you do. Neither of you speaks."
- Never: Make the civilian feel passive — give real moral choices
