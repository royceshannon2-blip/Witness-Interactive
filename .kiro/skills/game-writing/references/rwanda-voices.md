# Rwanda Character Voices

## Immaculée (Tutsi Survivor, age 19, university student)
**In crisis:** Observational, precise. She notices the militia commander's age, which card is in which pocket, the smell of smoke. Terror is in the precision, not named.
**In aftermath (2006–2010):** Composed, holds contradictions without resolving them. "Both things were true at the same time" is her register.
**Never:** Wails, breaks down, becomes inspirational, becomes a lesson.

**Specific details locked to her story:**
- Marie's Hutu identity card — borrowed, never returned
- Jeanne's attic — four feet by six feet, 72 hours without standing
- Didier, age 8, questioned in the kitchen below her
- Paul killed at a roadblock April 12th — five days after she hid
- Emmanuel — old man in church ceiling, retired teacher, gave her his last water, died four days after liberation
- Théoneste — Jeanne's Interahamwe brother, staffed Paul's roadblock

## Augustin (Hutu Moderate, communal secretary, ~age 40)
**In crisis:** Bureaucratic and procedural even under pressure. Thinks in records and lists. When he breaks from procedure, it costs something visible.
**In aftermath:** Carries guilt in silence. Does not perform remorse. Does what is required.
**Never:** Explains why he did what he did, asks for forgiveness, becomes a redemption arc.

**Specific details locked to his story:**
- Celestin — Tutsi neighbor he hid, whose wife and children did not survive
- Patrick — Celestin's son, now 23, walks past Augustin's house
- The gacaca hearing where Celestin is a judge watching Augustin testify
- Eight hundred hours rebuilding the school where Paul taught

## Captain Marcus Webb (UN Peacekeeper, Canadian Forces, UNAMIR, ~age 35)
**In crisis:** Military precision. Short declarative sentences. Assesses situations — assets, threats, time. Does not editorialize.
**In aftermath (Canadian inquiry 2004, return to Kigali 2010):** Same precision now applied to guilt. Accounts for himself without sentimentality.
**Never:** Hero narrative. Both staying and leaving carry weight.

**Specific details locked to his story:**
- General Roméo Dallaire — Webb's commander, *Shake Hands with the Devil* (2003)
- The genocide fax — January 1994 warning to New York, ignored
- UNAMIR cut from 2,500 to 270 after 10 Belgians killed
- Rusesabagina's negotiation and his 2021 terrorism conviction (separate conflict — not 1994)
- Responsibility to Protect — passed UN 2005
- The woman at the checkpoint holding a child toward the vehicle window
- Canadian Senate Special Committee on Rwanda (2004)

## Historical Terms — Gloss on First Use
| Term | How to introduce it |
|------|---------------------|
| RTLM | "a radio station that spent months calling Tutsi 'cockroaches' and directing militia to kill them" |
| Interahamwe | "Hutu extremist militia groups organized specifically to carry out killings" |
| RPF | "the Tutsi rebel force that ended the genocide by capturing Kigali in July 1994" |
| Gacaca | "community-level justice courts where perpetrators could confess in exchange for reduced sentences — Rwanda ran roughly 12,000 of them from 2005 to 2012" |
| ICTR | "the International Criminal Tribunal for Rwanda, the international court set up in Arusha to prosecute genocide organizers" |
| UNAMIR | "the UN peacekeeping mission in Rwanda, given a monitor-only mandate and no enforcement powers" |
| Kwibuka | "'remember' in Kinyarwanda — Rwanda's annual April 7th national genocide remembrance" |
