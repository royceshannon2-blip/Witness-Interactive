# Game Writing Skill

Write cinematic, emotionally resonant, historically grounded scene content. Every word serves both immersion and AP learning.

## Core Rules
- Second person present tense always: "You grip the railing" not "He gripped"
- Never head-hop — stay in the player's immediate sensory experience
- Never summarize what the player feels — describe what they sense
- Short sentences during crisis. Long sentences during reflection.
- Never use: "Suddenly", "You feel", "You think to yourself", "Little did you know", "History would later show", "You can't believe"

## Scene Length
- Narrative text: 80–150 words maximum
- Choice text: 4–8 words, urgent, present tense
- Consequence text: 15–25 words, immediate, never resolve the tension

## Scene Structure
1. Opening line: drop into the middle of the moment, no setup
2. Sensory build: 2–3 sentences, at least 3 of 5 senses (sound, smell, touch, sight, taste)
3. Situation: 1–2 sentences establishing the decision context
4. Tension: 1 sentence raising stakes

## Good Choices
- Both options must feel viable — no obvious right answer
- Must reflect real historical tensions
- Must connect to the AP theme of the scene
- No option should feel like the "game optimal" play
