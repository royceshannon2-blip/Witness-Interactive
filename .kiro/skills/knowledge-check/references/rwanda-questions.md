## Tutsi Survivor — Immaculée

**Q1 (early game):**
"When Immaculée arrived at the church, what made it feel safe?"
- ✓ Father Michel was there, hundreds of Tutsi families had gathered, and churches had always been sanctuary
- ✗ UN peacekeepers had established a checkpoint at the church entrance (UN Peacekeeper path)
- ✗ Her friend Jeanne had arranged for Father Michel to hide her specifically (Jeanne path detail)
- ✗ The Interahamwe commander announced the church was neutral territory (Hutu Moderate path parallel)

**Q2 (mid game):**
"Why was Jeanne's attic dangerous even though it provided shelter?"
- ✓ Jeanne's brother Théoneste was Interahamwe and came to the house every day — she had to lie to him repeatedly
- ✗ The UN convoy parked outside made the house a target for militia searching for collaborators (UN path)
- ✗ Augustin had flagged the house on the communal secretary's suspect list (Hutu Moderate path)
- ✗ The hotel manager had told militia which Hutu houses were sheltering survivors (hotel path)

**Q3 (aftermath):**
"What did Immaculée discover at the gacaca hearing in 2007 that she hadn't known before?"
- ✓ That Théoneste — Jeanne's brother — had staffed the roadblock where Paul was killed on April 12th
- ✗ That Captain Webb had known about the roadblock but couldn't intervene under his mandate (UN path)
- ✗ That Augustin had identified Paul's card at a checkpoint before the roadblock (Hutu Moderate path)
- ✗ That Father Michel had survived the church massacre and testified at gacaca (invented but plausible)

## Hutu Moderate — Augustin

**Q1 (early game):**
"What role did Augustin hold that put him in an impossible position when the genocide began?"
- ✓ Communal secretary — he had access to population records and was expected to help coordinate roadblocks
- ✗ Hotel manager — he was responsible for the Tutsi sheltering in the building (hotel path)
- ✗ UN liaison officer — he had to translate orders between UNAMIR and local officials (UN path)
- ✗ Church deacon — he had promised Father Michel he would protect the building (church path)

**Q2 (mid game):**
"What made Augustin's situation at the roadblock morally complicated?"
- ✓ He checked identity cards and followed orders while secretly misdirecting searches and helping Celestin hide
- ✗ He confiscated Tutsi identity cards and replaced them with false Hutu cards from embassy workers (hotel path adaptation)
- ✗ He documented which commanders gave orders, collecting evidence for future tribunals (UN documentation path)
- ✗ He allowed people through without checking cards until a militia commander directly observed him (invented plausible)

**Q3 (aftermath):**
"What was Augustin's community service sentence after his gacaca confession?"
- ✓ Eight hundred hours rebuilding the school where Paul — Jeanne's husband killed at Théoneste's roadblock — had taught
- ✗ Three years working at the Hôtel des Mille Collines under supervision of survivor families (hotel path)
- ✗ Twelve months assisting the ICTR in Arusha as a document translator (UN/ICTR path)
- ✗ Six months at a reconciliation center founded by survivors of the church massacre (church path)

## UN Peacekeeper — Captain Webb

**Q1 (early game):**
"What was UNAMIR's official mandate and why did it matter?"
- ✓ Monitor and report only — no enforcement powers — Webb could not intervene to stop killings even when he witnessed them
- ✗ Protect all civilians within 2km of UN positions, but the mandate excluded religious sites (invented, plausible)
- ✗ Full peace enforcement — but the Security Council secretly revoked it on April 6th (invented)
- ✗ Evacuate foreign nationals and UN-designated refugees, excluding Rwandan civilians (partially true but wrong framing)

**Q2 (mid game):**
"Why did the militia not attack the hotel immediately after the UN withdrawal order?"
- ✓ Webb's blue helmets maintained a visible presence the militia was reluctant to challenge — they were waiting for full withdrawal
- ✗ Rusesabagina had paid the militia commander's salary directly from hotel funds (partially documented, wrong as complete answer)
- ✗ The RPF had already entered Kigali and militia commanders were receiving conflicting orders (incorrect timeline)
- ✗ Immaculée and other survivors had organized a communication system alerting RPF to the threat (Tutsi Survivor path adapted)

**Q3 (aftermath):**
"Why did Webb's documentation matter years after the genocide, even though it didn't stop the killing?"
- ✓ His photographs and timestamped field reports helped the ICTR's Media Trial establish that broadcast orders directly preceded specific massacres — key precedents in genocide law
- ✗ His records were used by gacaca courts to assign individual sentences to roadblock commanders (gacaca was community-level, not ICTR)
- ✗ His testimony at Augustin's gacaca hearing confirmed the communal secretary had cooperated with UNAMIR (Hutu Moderate path)
- ✗ His photographs were primary evidence in Rusesabagina's 2021 terrorism trial (different conflict entirely)

- [ ] Correct answer requires playing this specific role — not general historical knowledge

- [ ] All 4 answers feel historically plausible to someone who hasn't played this role
- [ ] Question tests understanding, not trivia
- [ ] No moral judgment embedded in the correct answer
