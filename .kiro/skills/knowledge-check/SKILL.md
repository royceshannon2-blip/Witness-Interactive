# Knowledge Check Skill

Every knowledge checkpoint question in Witness Interactive tests what a student learned by playing their specific role — not general historical knowledge. Wrong answers are drawn from real moments in the other roles, making them feel plausible but requiring actual play to distinguish.

## Core Design Rule
**Correct answer:** Only knowable by playing this specific role's scenes.
**Wrong answers:** Real events from the other two roles — historically accurate, but not what happened in your path.

## Question Structure
- 3 questions per role
- Q1 (early game): Tests a decision from scenes 01–02
- Q2 (mid game): Tests a decision from scenes 02–03
- Q3 (aftermath): Tests a consequence from epilogue/outcome scenes
- All 4 answer options feel plausible to someone who hasn't played this role
- No moral judgment embedded in the correct answer
- No trivia — tests understanding of cause and consequence

## Quality Checklist
Before finalizing any question:
- [ ] Correct answer requires playing this specific role — not general history knowledge
- [ ] All 3 wrong answers are drawn from real moments in other roles (not invented)
- [ ] All 4 answers feel historically plausible to someone who hasn't played this role
- [ ] Question tests understanding, not trivia
- [ ] No moral judgment embedded in correct answer
