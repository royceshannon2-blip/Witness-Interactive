# Pearl Harbor Verified Facts

## Timeline
- 3:42 AM: USS Condor spots midget submarine
- 6:37 AM: USS Ward sinks midget submarine — first US shots of WWII
- 6:53 AM: Ward's report reaches naval command, ignored
- 7:02 AM: Radar operators detect incoming aircraft
- 7:20 AM: Lt. Tyler dismisses contact as B-17s from mainland
- 7:48 AM: First wave attack begins — 183 aircraft
- 7:55 AM: USS Arizona struck, forward magazine explodes
- 8:10 AM: USS Arizona sinks, 1,177 crew killed
- 8:54 AM: Second wave — 170 aircraft
- 9:55 AM: Attack ends

## Japanese Force (Kido Butai)
- Commander: Vice Admiral Chuichi Nagumo
- 6 carriers: Akagi, Kaga, Soryu, Hiryu, Shokaku, Zuikaku
- 353 total aircraft, two waves
- First wave commander: Commander Mitsuo Fuchida
- Signal "Tora! Tora! Tora!" confirmed complete surprise
- Japanese losses: 29 aircraft, 5 midget submarines, 64 men

## American Casualties
- Total: 2,403 killed, 1,178 wounded
- Aircraft destroyed: 188, damaged: 159
- Ships sunk/damaged: 8 battleships, 3 cruisers, 3 destroyers
- USS Arizona: 1,177 killed — still on harbor floor
- USS Oklahoma: capsized, 429 killed
- Carriers USS Enterprise, Lexington, Saratoga: at sea, undamaged

## Geography
- Pearl Harbor: Oahu, Hawaii — US territory in 1941, not yet a state
- Battleship Row: southeast side of Ford Island
- Hickam Field: adjacent Army Air Corps base
- Wheeler Field: central Oahu fighter base

## Civilian Context
- Honolulu population: ~180,000 in 1941
- Japanese-American community: ~37% of Hawaii's population
- Martial law declared: 11:30 AM December 7, 1941
- Some civilian casualties from misdirected US anti-aircraft shells
