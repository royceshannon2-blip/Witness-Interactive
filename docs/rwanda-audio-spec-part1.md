# Rwanda Mission Audio Specification - Part 1: Hutu Moderate Role

## Overview
This document specifies all audio requirements for the Rwanda Genocide mission (1994). Audio is organized by role and scene, with priority ratings and sourcing notes.

## Audio Sourcing Notes
- **Existing Pearl Harbor audio**: We have explosion, aircraft, and ocean wave sounds that may be reusable
- **Rwanda-specific needs**: RTLM radio broadcasts, machete sounds, African ambient sounds, Kinyarwanda language
- **Trauma-informed approach**: Violence is implied through distant sounds, never graphic or explicit

---

## ROLE 1: HUTU MODERATE (Augustin)

### Scene: rw-hm-scene-01
**Context**: April 6, 1994 evening. Radio announces plane crash. Neighbor seeks shelter.

**Sound 1: RTLM Radio Broadcast (Hate Speech)**
- Type: Ambient loop
- Description: Muffled radio broadcast in Kinyarwanda/French, agitated announcer tone, propaganda music stings
- Mood: Tense, ominous
- Priority: HIGH
- Notes: Should sound like it's coming from a radio in another room. Mix of speech and music. Not graphic content, but urgent/angry tone. 15-30 second loop.
- Pearl Harbor overlap: None

**Sound 2: Distant Vehicle Convoy**
- Type: Ambient loop
- Description: Truck engines, multiple vehicles moving through city streets, distant
- Mood: Tense, threatening
- Priority: MEDIUM
- Notes: Should feel like military/militia movement. 1-2 blocks away. Layer with occasional shouts (unintelligible).
- Pearl Harbor overlap: Could adapt truck sounds if we have military vehicle audio

**Sound 3: Urban Night Ambient (Kigali)**
- Type: Ambient loop
- Description: Distant dogs barking, occasional voices, city hum, insects
- Mood: Eerie, tense
- Priority: LOW
- Notes: African urban soundscape. Sparse, not dense. Creates unease.
- Pearl Harbor overlap: None


### Scene: rw-hm-scene-02a
**Context**: Morning. Militia at door searching for hidden Tutsi. Floorboard creaks above.

**Sound 1: Crowd/Militia Outside**
- Type: Ambient loop
- Description: 15-20 male voices, some chanting, machetes tapping on pavement, boots on gravel
- Mood: Threatening, chaotic
- Priority: HIGH
- Notes: Should sound like organized mob. Rhythmic machete taps create menace. Voices in Kinyarwanda (unintelligible). 20-30 second loop.
- Pearl Harbor overlap: None

**Sound 2: Heavy Knock on Door**
- Type: One-shot
- Description: Aggressive pounding on wooden door, 3-4 knocks
- Mood: Threatening
- Priority: HIGH
- Notes: Should make player jump. Loud, urgent, aggressive.
- Pearl Harbor overlap: Generic door knock could work

**Sound 3: Creaking Floorboard**
- Type: One-shot
- Description: Single wooden floorboard creak from above
- Mood: Tense
- Priority: MEDIUM
- Notes: Subtle but audible. Creates "oh no" moment. Should sound like someone shifting weight.
- Pearl Harbor overlap: None

### Scene: rw-hm-scene-02b
**Context**: Evening rally. Hutu Power organizer recruiting for roadblocks.

**Sound 1: Rally Crowd**
- Type: Ambient loop
- Description: 200+ men, chanting, clapping, aggressive energy, call-and-response
- Mood: Chaotic, aggressive
- Priority: HIGH
- Notes: Should feel like political rally turning violent. Rhythmic chanting. Occasional cheers. 30-45 second loop.
- Pearl Harbor overlap: None

**Sound 2: RTLM Radio (Rally Background)**
- Type: Ambient loop
- Description: Radio playing in background, music and announcer, celebratory tone
- Mood: Eerie (cheerful tone during genocide)
- Priority: MEDIUM
- Notes: Same as scene 01 but more upbeat/celebratory. Creates disturbing contrast.
- Pearl Harbor overlap: None


### Scene: rw-hm-scene-02c
**Context**: Pre-dawn. Fleeing Kigali. Smoke, screaming, chaos.

**Sound 1: Distant Screaming**
- Type: Ambient loop
- Description: Distant human screams (not graphic), 2-3 blocks away, occasional, mixed with shouting
- Mood: Terrifying, chaotic
- Priority: HIGH
- Notes: TRAUMA-INFORMED: Screams should be distant, brief, implied. Never graphic or prolonged. Creates atmosphere without explicit violence.
- Pearl Harbor overlap: Could adapt distant civilian panic sounds if available

**Sound 2: Machete Sounds (Distant)**
- Type: Ambient loop
- Description: Metallic clanging, machetes on metal/pavement, rhythmic, 1-2 blocks away
- Mood: Threatening, ominous
- Priority: HIGH
- Notes: RWANDA-SPECIFIC: Machetes were primary weapon. Sound should be metallic, rhythmic, menacing. Never sounds of impact on people.
- Pearl Harbor overlap: None

**Sound 3: Urban Fire/Smoke**
- Type: Ambient loop
- Description: Crackling fire, distant, smoke hiss, occasional collapse sounds
- Mood: Chaotic, destructive
- Priority: MEDIUM
- Notes: Buildings burning. Should feel widespread but not immediate danger.
- Pearl Harbor overlap: Could adapt fire sounds from Pearl Harbor if available

### Scene: rw-hm-scene-03a
**Context**: Roadblock. Checking identity cards. Militia watching.

**Sound 1: Roadblock Ambient**
- Type: Ambient loop
- Description: Truck engine idling, militia voices (low conversation), occasional radio chatter, machetes tapping
- Mood: Tense, methodical
- Priority: HIGH
- Notes: Should feel organized, bureaucratic, menacing. Not chaotic - this is systematic genocide.
- Pearl Harbor overlap: Truck idle could be adapted

**Sound 2: Machete Tapping on Truck Bed**
- Type: Ambient loop (rhythmic)
- Description: Metallic tapping, 2-3 machetes, irregular rhythm, patient but threatening
- Mood: Menacing, patient
- Priority: HIGH
- Notes: Creates countdown feeling. Militia is waiting, watching. Rhythmic but not musical.
- Pearl Harbor overlap: None
